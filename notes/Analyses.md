## Republic

### Book 1

- Polemarchus:
    - Proposition: Justice is doing good to friends and harm to enemies
    - Refutation: Justice is to treat something rightly. Like treating a horse to improve it's horse-ness. And doing something to a person that increases his capacity/virtue as a person. But, to harm a person is to diminish him as a person. So that cannot be justice. QED

- Thrasymachus:
    - Proposition: Justice is the advantage of the strong
    - Socrates' refutation: justice turns out to be the advantage of the subject not the art
    - Proposition: Justice is well-bred simplemindedness, injustice is goodness of judgement
    - Socrates' refutation: A just man/city/group is not at odds with itself nor with other just entities and so the just entity is capable of action. Whereas, a purely unjust entity is completely impotent.
